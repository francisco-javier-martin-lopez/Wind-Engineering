# Wind Engineering: Crosswind Effects on Vehicle Dynamic Stability

This repository contains the presentation and research materials developed for the "Wind Engineering" course at Politecnico di Milano (Academic Year 2024/2025). The study investigates the dynamic stability and rollover risk of heavy transport vehicles (trucks) subjected to severe crosswind conditions.

**Team:** Co-developed by Alejandro Rivera Míguez, Mikel Segovia Díaz, and Francisco Javier Martín López.

### Project Overview & Methodology

* **Objective:** To conduct a comprehensive risk assessment of truck rollover thresholds by coupling aerodynamic loads with vehicle dynamics, evaluating how wind intensity, payload, and driving maneuvers interact.
* **Environmental Variables:** Simulated extreme weather scenarios by testing different crosswind velocities (25 and 30 m/s) combined with varying turbulence intensities (7%, 14%, and 25%).
* **Vehicle Parameterization:** Analyzed the sensitivity of the vehicle's stability to structural and loading changes, specifically focusing on the payload fraction ($\lambda$) and the roll stiffness distribution ($\tau$).
* **Transient Maneuver Simulation:** Evaluated the truck's dynamic response across multiple critical driving scenarios, including:
  * Straight-line driving under lateral aerodynamic loads.
  * Aggressive cornering (steady-state left and right turns).
  * Transient overtaking maneuvers (single and double lane changes).
* **Key Findings:** Correlated critical rollover speeds with the payload fraction and turning aggressiveness, mapping the truck's trajectory and stability limits to provide actionable insights for road safety and vehicle design.
